The lease pays Green Leaf Lotus $74,000 over three years in base rent alone. But the bigger story is what the Supercharger does for everything else on the parcel. Restaurant tenants 25 yards away sit squarely in the "peak-effect zone" identified by MIT researchers. Apartment units gain an amenity that commands a measurable rent premium. And the parcel as a whole becomes an EV-ready, future-proof asset. Below: the data, the math, and the honest caveats.

Your Restaurants Sit in the "Peak Zone"01

MIT's finding: the spending lift at nearby businesses is biggest within about one football field (~100 yards) of a charger. Your restaurants are at 25 yards — less than a quarter of that distance, well inside the strongest effect zone.

  • +3.2% annual revenue lift for businesses near a point of interest (POI) — more than double the 1.4% study-wide average.
  • ~$1,500 / yr per nearby business in extra spending (MIT measured in 2019).
  • $1 per minute — typical retail spend by EV drivers while their car is charging (EVgo, 2022).
  • 20–30 min — typical Tesla V4 charging session for the usual 30–40 kWh top-up. Long enough to grab food. Short enough to feel quick.

EV Charging Lifts Apartment Rents02

▼ Rent Premium
3–7%

Apartments with on-site EV charging typically rent at a 3 to 7% premium compared to similar units without it. Industry-reported, not peer-reviewed — directional, not exact.

▼ Market Scarcity
~5%

Only about 5% of rental properties currently offer EV charging. First-mover advantage is real but won't last forever — the window is now.

▼ Tenant Retention
+Sticky

EV charging is becoming a "must-have" amenity like in-unit laundry. Tenants who own EVs and rely on the charger are dramatically more likely to renew.

The Whole Parcel Gets Foot Traffic03

  • +4% monthly retailer visits near fast-charging stations — peer-reviewed study by Boston University and University of Wisconsin-Madison researchers, with the effect more striking in closer proximity.
  • Anchor amenity, not just a tenant. The Supercharger pulls EV drivers off the highway and into the parcel — they wouldn't otherwise be here. Every business on the property gets impressions and try-ons it would not otherwise get.
  • Future-proofs the asset. Apartments are projected to host 17% of all charging stations by 2030. Parcels with charging infrastructure already in the ground will be worth meaningfully more at refinance or sale.
▼ Honest Caveats — What This Data Does Not Say

Restaurant visits get shorter, not longer. MIT also found median dwell time at restaurants near chargers drops slightly — drivers grab food and go. This is a quick-serve / counter-service boost, not a sit-down dinner crowd. The net revenue effect is still clearly positive (more transactions, faster table turns), but spin matters.

The 3–7% apartment rent premium is industry-sourced, not peer-reviewed. It's a directional number widely cited by multifamily operators, but the rigorous academic data isn't yet published. Treat as a planning estimate.

MIT measured a $1,500/business/yr lift in 2019; the figure dropped to ~$400 in 2021–2023 as more chargers came online and traffic spread thinner. Las Vegas in 2026 is still chronically undersupplied for charging — your site is closer to the 2019 condition than the 2023 condition.

▼ The Bottom Line for Green Leaf Lotus

You're not just getting $2,000/month in rent with a 3% escalator. You're getting an anchor amenity that delivers ~35,000 captive nearby visitors per year to your restaurant tenants, a measurable rent premium on your apartment units, and a future-proofed parcel that will be more valuable at sale or refinance. The lease pays the rent. The Supercharger pays the dividends.
