03Lease Protections for Both Sides

Long deals need clear rules. These 4 lease terms protect both parties; if any are missing, neither side should sign.

1. SNDA from your lender Three-way promise signed by you, your lender, and Future Tech. If anything ever happens to your mortgage, the lease still stands — protects your rent stream from forced termination, protects our equipment investment.
2. Equipment ownership clarity The chargers are filed with the state as Future Tech 9000 property. Keeps them off your real estate tax assessment and out of your mortgage collateral — and clearly identifies who owns and maintains what.
3. Lease recorded at the county A short summary filed at Clark County. Public proof of the lease terms for both parties, in case either side ever sells or refinances. $45 fee.
4. Cure rights If either side messes up, the other gets written notice and a chance to fix it before the lease can be terminated. 30 days for money issues, 60+ for everything else. Equal for both parties.
