AnsweredFor who? Tesla offers no exclusivity. So, someone else can place more chargers on the other side of the street.
What are termination options and penalties?
New answerProposed framework (final terms in lease draft):
• Default cure period: 30 days for monetary default, 60 days for non-monetary, with notice.
• Voluntary early termination by landlord: requires buyout of unamortized capital improvements (declining balance over the lease term).
• Voluntary early termination by operator: equipment is removed at our cost; site is restored.
• Force majeure / regulatory: standard suspension clauses.
Specific dollar amounts and notice periods to be drafted by counsel.
Can we buy out the equipment later? At what price?
AnsweredYes but this would encapsulate the equipment and future revenue for a fair market valuation. At this time we don't believe this would be a simple transaction.
What happens if Operating Company is acquired or goes out of business?
New answer • Acquisition: the lease and operating rights transfer to the acquirer. Standard assignment provisions in the lease protect Green Leaf's revshare and minimum payment.
• Bankruptcy: the lease becomes an asset of the estate; landlord has step-in rights to either (a) keep the chargers operating with a successor operator or (b) terminate the lease and take title to the equipment at fair-market value.
• Landlord protection: a UCC filing and equipment lien framework will be specified so Green Leaf is not exposed to operator creditors.
What happens when Green Leaf Lotus is sold?
AnsweredTransfer of lease to new owners. Just like your commercial leases.
