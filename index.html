⚖️ Legal & Risk — Plain English
What could go wrong, how bad it is, and exactly what to do about it.
Risk Radar — Read This Before Writing Any Checks
🔴 HIGH

You Don't Own The Land

You're putting $1.4 million into a station on someone else's property. If that property gets sold or the owner goes bankrupt, your station could be at risk. Fix: Get an SNDA agreement signed and in writing before a single shovel goes in the ground. This is the most important legal document in the whole deal.

🔴 HIGH

Tesla Controls The Network Rules

You own the physical hardware, but Tesla runs the software, sets the pricing, and handles billing. If Tesla changes its fees or shuts down its business program, your income is affected. Fix: Negotiate your Tesla contract carefully. Get your exit terms, fee caps, and notice periods in writing.

🟡 MEDIUM

Someone Gets Hurt at Your Station

If a driver slips, gets shocked by equipment, or has any accident at your station, they can sue you. Fix: Set up a separate LLC for each station, and get at least $2M in general liability insurance. Two layers of protection = your personal savings stay safe.

🟡 MEDIUM

Self-Driving Laws Are Still Being Written

Robotaxis are brand new. State and federal governments are still figuring out the rules. A law change could slow down Cybercab operations in certain cities. Fix: Balance your investment between cabs and stations. Stations work whether robotaxis are allowed or not.

🟡 MEDIUM

Tax Laws and Credits Could Change

The 30% solar tax credit and depreciation rules are controlled by Congress. They've changed before. Fix: Build your profit model assuming zero tax credits. If you get them, treat it as a bonus — not a requirement.

🟢 LOW

Slow Start in Year 1

A brand-new station takes time to build a customer base. Your loan payment still comes due every month. Fix: Keep 6 months of loan payments in a cash reserve account per station (~$62,000). This is your safety net.

🟢 LOW

Owner Disagreements

Money and decisions can create friction between partners. Fix: Write an Operating Agreement before anyone invests a single dollar. It spells out who decides what, how profit is split, and how someone can exit.

✅ Your 7-Step Protection Checklist
  • 1️⃣
    Get an SNDA Agreement — Before Construction Starts This document (Subordination, Non-Disturbance & Attornment) is a legal promise that your lease stays protected even if the property is sold or the owner goes broke. No SNDA = no deal.
  • 2️⃣
    Form a Separate LLC For Each Station A Limited Liability Company keeps each station's risks separated from your personal money and from each other. If one station has a problem, the others are protected.
  • 3️⃣
    Record Your Lease at the County Courthouse For about $200, you can file your lease in public records. This puts the entire world on legal notice that you have rights to this property. Cheap insurance.
  • 4️⃣
    Get $2M+ Liability Insurance Per Station General liability covers injuries and accidents. Also get property and equipment coverage. Budget $8,000–$15,000 per station per year. Non-negotiable.
  • 5️⃣
    Write a Clear Investor Operating Agreement The rulebook for your group. Who votes on big decisions? How is profit split? How does someone exit? What happens if someone wants to sell their share? Write this first.
  • 6️⃣
    Negotiate Your Tesla Contract Before Signing Understand: What can Tesla change, and with how much notice? What happens if you want to sell the station? What are the termination terms? Get a lawyer to review it.
  • 7️⃣
    Keep 6 Months of Payments in Cash Reserve Per station: ~$62,250 in the bank. Per 100 cabs: ~$350,000. This protects you in the ramp-up phase when income is still growing. Think of it as your parachute.
📖 Legal Words Explained Simply
SNDA Agreement
A legal paper that says your lease is protected even if the building owner sells the property or goes bankrupt. The new owner must honor your lease.
LLC (Limited Liability Company)
A type of business that protects your personal money. If the business gets sued or owes money, they can't come after your house or savings.
Recording a Lease
Filing your lease at the county records office so it's part of the official public record. Makes it very hard for someone to claim they didn't know about your rights.
Memorandum of Lease
A short summary of your lease that you record publicly, instead of the full document. Protects your privacy while still putting your rights on record.
Premises Liability
If someone gets hurt at your property, you can be responsible. That's why LLC + insurance is so important — two shields between a lawsuit and your personal money.
Non-Disturbance Agreement
Part of an SNDA — a promise from the bank or lender that holds a mortgage on the property. They agree that even if they take the building, your lease stays.
